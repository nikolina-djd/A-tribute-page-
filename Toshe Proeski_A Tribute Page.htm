Toshe Proeski

1981-2007

Top act of the local Macedonian and Balkan music scene

Todor "Toše" Proeski (Macedonian: Тодор "Тоше" Проески, pronounced [ˈtɔʃɛ ˈprɔɛski] (About this soundlisten); 25 January 1981 – 16 October 2007) was a Macedonian multi-genre singer, songwriter and actor. Considered a top act of the local Macedonian and Balkan music scene, Proeski's music was popular across multitude of countries of Southeast Europe. He was dubbed the "Elvis Presley of the Balkans" by BBC News.

The following list is a time line of Toshe Proeski

  • 1981 born in Prilep
  • 1996 First public music performancei. At the age of 12, he was chosen to perform at the popular children's song festival Zlatno Slavejče
  • 1997 He participated in the music festival Makfest in Štip with the song "Pušti me" ("Let Me Go")
  • 2000 Proeski participated in the Eurovision pre-selection for Macedonia in SkopjeFest. He sang "Solzi pravat zlaten prsten" ("Tears Make a Golden Ring"), which won the televoting from the public
  • 2001 Proeski embarked on an Australian tour along with other Macedonian singers
  • 2002 After spending his time in recording studios in Athens, Greece, Proeski released his third album "Ako me pogledneš vo oči" ("If You Look into My Eyes") in October 2002 in Macedonian and Serbian
  • 2003 He was awarded with the Mother Theresa Humanitarian Award and in 2003 he became a Regional UNICEF Ambassador.
  • 2004 MKTV chose Proeski to represent Macedonia at the Eurovision Song Contest 2004 in Istanbul, Turkey
  • 2004 Proeski was named a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador, and recorded the song "This World" which became the UNICEF anthem
  • 2005 Toše's fifth album Po tebe (After You) was released throughout ex-Yugoslavia.
  • 2006 He performed his second solo concert at the City Stadium in Skopje.
  • 2007 Toše' last album Igri bez granici (Macedonian title)/Igra bez granica (Serbo-Croatian title) ("Game Without Limits") was released all over ex-Yugoslavia in August. He for the Croatian Radio Festival with his song Veži me za sebe won a Grand Prix award in the category for pop-rock and urban music with 16.589 points. On October 5th, 2007 at 07:30 pm, performed his spectacular third and final concert, again on the City Stadium in Skopje, supported by the organization USAID.
  • 2007 He died in a car crash on the Zagreb–Lipovac A3 highway, near Nova Gradiška in Croatia, on the morning of 16 October 2007, aged 26.
